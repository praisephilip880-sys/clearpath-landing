Questions

Before you ask

Does this guarantee I won't get sued?

No tool can promise that. What it does is close the most common, easily detectable gaps that plaintiff's firms specifically look for, and keeps closing new ones as your site changes — which is the proactive posture that courts and lawyers consistently point to as the right approach.

Is this the same as one of those accessibility widgets?

No. Overlay widgets sit on top of your site and don't fix the underlying code — they're widely viewed as insufficient and have themselves drawn lawsuits. Clearpath finds the real, specific problems in your actual code so you or your developer can fix them.

Do I need a developer to use this?

To read the reports, no. To fix some issues, often yes — though many flagged problems (missing alt text, low contrast, missing form labels) are simple enough to fix yourself once you know exactly where they are.

What does it actually check?

WCAG 2.1 AA criteria — the same standard referenced in most ADA website lawsuits and DOJ guidance — covering things like color contrast, form labels, alt text, heading structure, and keyboard navigation.
